## What it does

The **ME Performance Analyzer** turns main engine telemetry into a senior technical review. It evaluates fuel efficiency (SFOC variance), cylinder-by-cylinder condition, exhaust-temperature deviation, overhaul timing, and turbocharger health, then escalates anything a Technical Superintendent must own.

It pairs with the [ME Performance Data Collector](/skills/me-performance-data-collector): one fetches, one interprets.

## When to use it

- "How is the main engine?"
- "ME status"
- "Run ME review"
- "SFOC variance check"
- "Cylinder condition on POSUN"

## What it looks at

- Per-cylinder exhaust temperature and deviation from mean
- SFOC actual vs design at the current load
- Cylinder pressure, peak pressure, scavenge pressure
- Turbocharger speed, boost pressure, exhaust back-pressure
- Hours since last cylinder overhaul, top-end overhaul, and major overhaul
- Recent alarms and trips

## How it works

### 1. Cylinder uniformity

Even cylinder loading is essential. The analyzer computes deviation per cylinder:

$$
\Delta T_i = T_i - \bar{T}
$$

A cylinder with $|\Delta T_i| > 30\,°\text{C}$ is flagged WARNING; $|\Delta T_i| > 50\,°\text{C}$ is CRITICAL — typically pointing to fuel injector, exhaust valve, or piston ring issues.

### 2. SFOC variance

$$
\text{Variance \%} = \frac{\text{SFOC}_\text{actual} - \text{SFOC}_\text{design}}{\text{SFOC}_\text{design}} \times 100
$$

Compared at the engine's current load against the maker's curve. Sustained variance above 5% is flagged; above 8% triggers escalation.

### 3. Pressure checks

Peak combustion pressure, mean indicated pressure, and scavenge pressure are compared against the maker's spec for the operating point. Deviations point to specific failure modes (fuel system, exhaust system, scavenge air).

### 4. Turbocharger

Boost pressure too low for engine load + high exhaust back-pressure points to fouling. Falling boost with rising exhaust temps is the classic turbo-degradation signature.

### 5. Overhaul timing

Hours-to-next overhaul are computed against the maker's interval:

$$
H_\text{remaining} = H_\text{interval} - (H_\text{current} - H_\text{last overhaul})
$$

Tiering:

- $H_\text{remaining} < 0$ — overdue, CRITICAL
- $0 \leq H_\text{remaining} < 500$ — imminent, escalate
- $500 \leq H_\text{remaining} < 2000$ — port-call coordination
- $H_\text{remaining} \geq 2000$ — routine

## What the review contains

- Executive summary with overall ME status
- Cylinder uniformity table (per-cylinder deviation, severity)
- SFOC analysis (variance, trend, cost impact)
- Pressure analysis with likely cause for any deviation
- Turbocharger assessment
- Overhaul planning
- Recommendations prioritised by impact and timeline
- Escalation decision

## Escalation

Escalates to the Technical Superintendent when:

| Trigger | Severity |
|---------|----------|
| Cylinder deviation above 50 °C | CRITICAL |
| SFOC variance above 8% | HIGH |
| Cylinder overhaul overdue | CRITICAL |
| Turbocharger boost low + exhaust high | HIGH |
| Peak pressure outside maker spec | HIGH |

## Why script-driven

Cylinder-deviation logic, SFOC calculation, and pressure-spec lookups all live in the analysis script. Reviews stay consistent vessel-to-vessel and traceable to the underlying numbers.
