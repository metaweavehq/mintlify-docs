## What it does

The **Certificates Analyzer** examines a vessel's full certificate portfolio — flag, class, statutory, trade, and crew documents — and produces a senior-level compliance review. It evaluates expiry windows, issuing-authority coverage, renewal lead-time, and **PSC detention risk**, then escalates anything that could ground the vessel.

It pairs with the [Certificates Data Collector](/skills/certificates-data-collector). The collector fetches the latest certificate snapshot; the analyzer interprets it.

## When to use it

- "Review certificates for AQUILA"
- "Are the certs OK?"
- "Any renewal risk on POSUN?"
- "PSC exposure from certificates"
- "Cert compliance check"

## What it looks at

- Full certificate inventory by category (statutory, class, flag, trade, crew)
- Expiry dates and remaining validity for every certificate
- Issuing authority coverage (RO authorisation, flag-state requirements)
- Renewal lead-time vs typical processing windows
- Recent endorsements, suspensions, or dispensations

## How it works

### 1. Portfolio coverage

The analyzer maps the vessel's certificate set against the mandatory list for its flag, class, and trade. Missing certificates raise an immediate compliance flag.

### 2. Expiry-window classification

Certificates are bucketed by remaining validity:

| Window | Bucket | Action |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Already expired | CRITICAL | Vessel non-compliant — escalate |
| 0–30 days | HIGH | Renewal urgent — coordinate with class/flag |
| 31–90 days | MEDIUM | Renewal in planning |
| 91–180 days | LOW | Track |
| Beyond 180 days | OK | Routine |

### 3. Detention-risk scoring

Each finding contributes to a **PSC detention risk** score:

$$
\text{Risk score} = \sum_i \left( w_\text{type}(c_i) \times w_\text{window}(c_i) \right)
$$

Where each certificate $c_i$ is weighted by its type criticality (statutory &gt; class &gt; trade) and its expiry-window urgency. Long-lead-survey renewals get an additional multiplier because they can't be rushed at the next port. The composite maps to LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH / CRITICAL bands.

### 4. Cost &amp; coordination impact

For each upcoming renewal the review estimates:

- Survey requirement (in-port vs. underway, surveyor availability)
- Approximate cost range
- Operational impact (off-hire risk, port-call coordination)

## What the review contains

- Executive summary with overall compliance status
- Portfolio table — every certificate, validity, issuing authority
- Expiry timeline — upcoming renewals grouped by urgency window
- Detention-risk assessment with contributing factors
- Recommended actions, prioritized by deadline
- Escalation decision

## Escalation

The analyzer escalates to a Technical Superintendent or Marine Superintendent automatically when:

| Trigger | Severity |
|---------|----------|
| Any statutory certificate expired | CRITICAL |
| Mandatory certificate missing | CRITICAL |
| Multiple HIGH-window renewals (no plan) | HIGH |
| PSC detention-risk score = HIGH or CRITICAL | CRITICAL |
| RO authorisation lapsed | HIGH |

Escalation routes the case to the right superintendent with the review and recommended actions attached.
