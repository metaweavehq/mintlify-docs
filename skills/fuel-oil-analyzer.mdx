## What it does

The **Fuel Oil Analyzer** reviews everything that affects a vessel's fuel posture: bunker quality from lab reports, ROB (remaining onboard) position vs voyage demand, consumption trend vs design, cat-fines exposure, and stem-planning lead-time. It produces a senior review and escalates situations a Technical Superintendent must own — debunkering, supply risk, sustained over-consumption.

It pairs with the [Fuel Oil Data Collector](/skills/fuel-oil-data-collector): one fetches, one interprets.

## When to use it

- "Fuel consumption OK?"
- "Bunker quality?"
- "Check ROB"
- "Cat fines high?"
- "Review fuel data"

## What it looks at

- ROB position by tank and grade
- Days of fuel at current burn vs voyage and stem schedule
- Lab results: viscosity, density, sulphur, cat fines, water, sediment
- Daily consumption vs design / charter party
- Stem schedule and supplier coverage
- Recent debunkering or quality disputes

## How it works

### 1. ROB and supply analysis

For each grade the analyzer computes:

$$
\text{Days of fuel} = \frac{\text{ROB (MT)}}{\text{Daily burn (MT/day)}}
$$

A safety margin is compared against the next stem date:

| Days remaining | Tier |
|----------------|------|
| Below 7 with no stem arranged | CRITICAL |
| 7–14 with stem &gt;7 days out | HIGH |
| 14–30 routine planning | MEDIUM |
| Above 30 | OK |

### 2. Quality assessment

Each lab parameter is compared against engine spec:

- Viscosity, density — engine treatment compatibility
- Sulphur — regulatory compliance (ECA / global cap)
- Cat fines — engine-damage risk; debunkering threshold typically 60 ppm
- Water, sediment — fuel system blockage and combustion quality

A parameter outside spec is classified WARNING; CRITICAL when it exceeds the maker's hard limit.

### 3. Consumption variance

Daily consumption is compared to design at current load and weather conditions. Sustained variance over 10% is flagged.

### 4. Stem planning

The script checks whether the next stem is booked, the supplier is qualified, and lead-time covers ROB depletion.

## What the review contains

- Executive summary with overall fuel status
- ROB by grade with days-of-fuel calculation
- Lab-result review per parameter with verdicts
- Consumption trend with cost impact estimate
- Stem plan and supply margin
- Cat-fines and debunkering verdict where relevant
- Escalation decision

## Escalation

Escalates to the Technical Superintendent when:

| Trigger | Severity |
|---------|----------|
| Cat fines above 60 ppm | CRITICAL |
| Quality issues requiring debunkering | CRITICAL |
| ROB critical with no stem arranged (under 7 days) | CRITICAL |
| Sustained consumption variance above 10% | HIGH |
| Sulphur breach in ECA | CRITICAL |
