## What it does

The **PMS Analyzer** reviews a vessel's Planned Maintenance System status, evaluates compliance percentage and overdue exposure, identifies systemic patterns (the same job overdue across the fleet, single equipment with chronic backlog), and produces a senior maintenance review.

It pairs with the [PMS Data Collector](/skills/pms-data-collector): one fetches, one interprets.

## When to use it

- "How is maintenance?"
- "Any overdue jobs?"
- "PMS review"
- "Maintenance compliance check"
- "Overdue PMS list"

## What it looks at

- Total mandatory PMS jobs vs completed on time
- Open jobs by criticality (critical, major, routine)
- Age of overdue jobs
- Recurring chronically-overdue jobs
- Equipment systems with concentrated backlog
- Closure rate trend across periods

## How it works

### 1. Compliance percentage

$$
\text{Compliance \%} = \frac{\text{Jobs completed on schedule}}{\text{Mandatory jobs in period}} \times 100
$$

A vessel below the configured threshold (typically 90%) is flagged.

### 2. Criticality-weighted exposure

The analyzer weights overdue counts by criticality:

$$
\text{Exposure score} = 5 \cdot N_\text{critical} + 2 \cdot N_\text{major} + 1 \cdot N_\text{routine}
$$

A score above the threshold triggers escalation regardless of overall compliance — five overdue safety items beats 95% compliance on routine jobs.

### 3. Chronic-overrun detection

Jobs overdue for three or more periods point to a systemic gap (parts unavailability, training gap, scheduling fault) — not isolated slips. These get called out specifically.

### 4. Equipment-system clusters

Three or more overdue jobs concentrated on the same equipment family suggests a problem with that system or its planning.

## What the review contains

- Executive summary with compliance verdict
- Overdue table by criticality and age
- Chronic-overrun jobs (likely systemic)
- Equipment clusters
- Closure-rate trend
- Recommendations prioritised by criticality and impact
- Escalation decision

## Escalation

Escalates to a Technical Superintendent when:

| Trigger | Severity |
|---------|----------|
| CRITICAL job overdue at all | CRITICAL |
| Compliance below 80% | HIGH |
| Chronic overrun (3+ periods) on safety-critical job | CRITICAL |
| Equipment cluster (3+ jobs same system) | HIGH |
| Class item overdue past deadline | CRITICAL |
