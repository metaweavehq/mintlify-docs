## What it does

The **Financial Analyzer** turns raw vessel spend data into a senior budget review. It evaluates budget-vs-actual variance, isolates cost drivers, projects year-end position, and escalates significant overspend to the Technical Superintendent.

It pairs with the [Financial Data Collector](/skills/financial-data-collector): one fetches, one interprets.

## When to use it

- "Budget OK?"
- "Overspend?"
- "How are we doing on costs?"
- "Financial review"
- "Year-end projection"

## What it looks at

- Budget allocation by category (technical, crew, lube, victualling, port disbursements, repairs)
- Year-to-date actual spend by category
- Variance per category, both absolute and percentage
- Trend across recent periods (accelerating vs flattening)
- Specific large or unusual transactions
- Year-end forecast based on burn rate

## How it works

### 1. Budget-vs-actual variance

For every cost category the analyzer computes:

$$
\text{Variance \%} = \frac{\text{Actual} - \text{Budget}}{\text{Budget}} \times 100
$$

A category is flagged when variance exceeds the configured threshold (typically 10%).

### 2. Cost-driver identification

Within an over-budget category, the script ranks transactions and vendor concentrations to identify what is actually driving the variance — bulk repair, single high-value PO, sustained burn-rate, or freight cost.

### 3. Year-end projection

Burn-rate projection extrapolates current spend to year-end:

$$
\text{Forecast}_\text{YE} = \text{Actual}_\text{YTD} + \text{BurnRate} \times \text{MonthsRemaining}
$$

A forecast that breaches budget by more than the threshold triggers escalation.

### 4. Trend tier

Categories are classified as:

- **Improving** — variance narrowing period-on-period
- **Stable** — variance flat
- **Deteriorating** — variance widening (escalation candidate)

## What the review contains

- Executive summary with overall budget status
- Variance table by category with flags
- Top cost drivers per over-budget category
- Trend per category (improving / stable / deteriorating)
- Year-end forecast with confidence range
- Recommendations (re-forecast, cost-control, vendor renegotiation)
- Escalation decision

## Escalation

Escalates to the Technical Superintendent when:

| Trigger | Severity |
|---------|----------|
| Year-end forecast breach &gt;10% | HIGH |
| Year-end forecast breach &gt;25% | CRITICAL |
| Single category overspend &gt;25% | HIGH |
| Deteriorating trend across 3+ categories | HIGH |
| Unbudgeted spend on safety-critical items | CRITICAL |
