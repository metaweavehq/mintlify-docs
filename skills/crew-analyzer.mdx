## What it does

The **Crew Analyzer** reviews everything that determines whether a vessel is correctly manned and legally compliant: rank coverage, certificate validity, contract windows, planned reliefs, and rest-hour exposure. It produces a senior review with MLC-compliance verdict and operational-risk assessment.

It pairs with the [Crew Data Collector](/skills/crew-data-collector): one fetches, one interprets.

## When to use it

- "Crew OK?"
- "Manning issues?"
- "Check certificates"
- "Crew review on POSUN"
- "Relief planning status"

## What it looks at

- Manning roster vs minimum safe manning
- Certificate validity per officer (CoC, GMDSS, STCW endorsements, medicals)
- Contract end dates and overdue contracts
- Planned vs unplanned reliefs and lead-time
- Rest-hour records (MLC 2.4 windows)
- Nationality mix and language coverage

## How it works

### 1. Manning compliance

Each rank slot is checked against minimum safe manning. Missing or under-rank coverage triggers an immediate flag.

### 2. Certificate expiry

Officer certificates are bucketed by remaining validity:

| Window | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Already expired | Officer must not sail — escalate |
| 0–30 days | Renewal urgent — coordinate with crewing |
| 31–90 days | Renewal in planning |
| Beyond 90 days | Routine |

### 3. Contract pressure

Officers past or near contract end without a relief plan are flagged. Sustained over-contract triggers escalation under MLC fatigue concerns.

### 4. Relief logistics

Planned reliefs are evaluated for visa, travel, and quarantine lead-time vs port-call windows.

### 5. Rest-hour exposure

The analyzer enforces MLC 2.4 minimums:

$$
H_\text{rest} \geq 10 \text{ h in any 24 h window} \quad \text{and} \quad H_\text{rest} \geq 77 \text{ h in any 7-day window}
$$

Repeated breaches across a crew indicate fatigue risk and SMS-deficiency exposure during the next PSC.

## What the review contains

- Executive summary with manning compliance verdict
- Roster table with rank, certificate validity, contract end
- Certificate renewal timeline
- Relief plan with logistics blockers
- Rest-hour deviations and fatigue-risk score
- Recommendations prioritised by sail date
- Escalation decision

## Escalation

Escalates to the Marine Superintendent automatically when:

| Trigger | Severity |
|---------|----------|
| Manning shortfall vs minimum safe manning | CRITICAL |
| Officer certificate expired | CRITICAL |
| Officer past contract with no relief planned | HIGH |
| Sustained rest-hour breaches | HIGH |
| Visa/travel blocking imminent relief | HIGH |
