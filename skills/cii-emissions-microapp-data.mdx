## What it does

The **CII &amp; Emissions Data Collector** captures a complete emissions snapshot for a vessel by pulling the latest numbers from the Emissions microapp and recording dashboard screenshots alongside the structured data. The result is an evidence package any reviewer can audit — numbers and visuals that match.

It covers the three regulatory frameworks the microapp tracks:

- **CII** — IMO Carbon Intensity Indicator rating (A–E)
- **EU ETS** — EU Emissions Trading System carbon allowances
- **FuelEU** — FuelEU Maritime greenhouse-gas intensity compliance

The CII attained value is calculated as:

$$
\text{CII}_\text{attained} = \frac{\text{Total CO}_2 \text{ emissions (g)}}{\text{Capacity} \times \text{Distance (nm)}}
$$

The vessel's rating band (A–E) is determined by comparing the attained value against the IMO required CII for the year. EU ETS allowances and FuelEU GHG intensity follow analogous per-voyage and annual aggregations.

## What it captures

| Data | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Fleet summary | Year-level CII, EU ETS, and FuelEU totals across the whole fleet |
| Vessel annual summary | Attained vs required CII, CO₂, distance sailed, rating band |
| Voyage breakdown | Per-voyage emissions, distances, fuel mix |
| Dashboard visuals | Full-page screenshots of fleet overview, vessel detail, EU ETS, and FuelEU pages |

## How it works

1. Calls the Emissions microapp API for the requested year and vessel IMO.
2. Captures the structured response for fleet-wide, vessel-level, and voyage-level data.
3. Drives a headless browser through the four dashboard pages, capturing a full-page screenshot of each.
4. Bundles JSON + screenshots into a single timestamped evidence record under the vessel's emissions case folder.
5. Generates a plain-English summary describing the rating, attained/required values, and key voyages.

If the browser tooling is unavailable, the API data still produces a valid evidence package — visuals are simply skipped.

## What it produces

- **Structured emissions data** — fleet, vessel, and voyage-level numbers consumed by downstream skills
- **Dashboard screenshots** — visual proof that the numbers reported match what users see in the microapp
- **Plain-English summary** — vessel name, IMO, year, rating, headline figures, voyage count
- **Filing metadata** — collection timestamp, source, sequence number

## Why visuals matter

Numbers on their own are easy to dispute. Pairing each collection with screenshots of the live dashboard means any reviewer or auditor can verify, at a glance, that the recorded numbers came from the same source the user sees in production.
