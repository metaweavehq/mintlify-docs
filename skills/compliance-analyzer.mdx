## What it does

The **Compliance Analyzer** synthesises a vessel's compliance posture across the four regimes that drive commercial and operational risk:

- **PSC** — Port State Control inspection history
- **SIRE** — Ship Inspection Report Programme (oil major vetting)
- **VIR** — Vessel Inspection Reports (charterer audits)
- **CII** — Carbon Intensity Indicator rating

It evaluates inspection findings, repeat themes, vetting gaps, and rating slippage, then produces a senior review with a clear detention-risk and commercial-impact verdict.

## When to use it

- "PSC risk?"
- "Are we compliant?"
- "SIRE status on POSUN"
- "Compliance review"
- "Detention exposure"

## What it looks at

- Open and recently closed PSC deficiencies, by code and severity
- Detention history and findings recurrence
- SIRE / VIR observations and operator-response status
- CII rating trend (current vs prior years, attained vs required)
- Crew familiarity and SMS-related deficiencies

## How it works

### 1. Investigation matrix

Each finding is run through an investigation matrix that maps it to:

- Root-cause category (technical, procedural, training, documentation)
- Recurrence (first-time vs repeat across vessels)
- Commercial impact (vetting failure, off-hire risk, fuel-cost impact)
- Time-to-close

### 2. Detention-risk scoring

A weighted composite score combines four contributing factors:

$$
\text{Risk score} = w_1 D_\text{open} + w_2 H_\text{detention} + w_3 R_\text{repeat} + w_4 S_\text{sms}
$$

Where $D_\text{open}$ is open deficiencies in the next port's regime, $H_\text{detention}$ is detention-history recency, $R_\text{repeat}$ is repeat-finding clusters, and $S_\text{sms}$ is crew / SMS deficiency density. The composite maps to LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH / CRITICAL bands.

### 3. Commercial-impact scoring

A parallel rating captures vetting and charterer exposure — failed SIRE, observations open beyond response window, CII rating dropping toward E.

## What the review contains

- Executive summary with overall compliance status
- PSC analysis — open deficiencies, regime risk, detention probability
- SIRE / VIR analysis — observation count, severity, response status
- CII trend — attained vs required, year-over-year change, projected rating
- Root-cause themes across findings
- Recommendations prioritised by deadline and impact
- Escalation decision

## Escalation

Escalates automatically when:

| Trigger | Severity |
|---------|----------|
| Detention-risk score = HIGH or CRITICAL | CRITICAL |
| Recent detention (under 12 months) | CRITICAL |
| SIRE failure or unresolved major observation | HIGH |
| CII rating projected E | HIGH |
| Repeat PSC finding (3+ vessels, same code) | HIGH |
