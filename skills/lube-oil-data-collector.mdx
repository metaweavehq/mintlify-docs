## What it does

The **Lube Oil Data Collector** captures the latest lube oil laboratory analysis for a vessel — wear metals, TBN, viscosity, water content, contaminants per equipment — and files a timestamped snapshot. It pairs with the [Lube Oil Analyzer](/skills/lube-oil-analyzer): one fetches, one interprets.

## When to use it

- "Lube oil check"
- "LO check"
- "LO results"
- "Oil analysis for AQUILA"

## What it captures

For each lubricated equipment (main engine system oil, cylinder oil, AE crankcase, hydraulics, gearboxes):

- Wear metals: Fe, Cu, Pb, Sn, Cr, Al
- Additive metals: Zn, Ca, Mg, P
- Contaminants: water, fuel dilution, soot, silicon
- Viscosity at 40 °C and 100 °C
- TBN, TAN, oxidation
- Sample date and equipment running hours at sample

## How it works

1. Looks up the vessel by IMO.
2. Pulls the latest lab batch from the configured source.
3. Writes a timestamped evidence record under the vessel's lube oil case folder.
4. Updates the case so any reviewing agent can see new evidence is available.

## What it produces

- Structured lab data consumed by the analyzer
- Plain-English summary covering equipment count, sample dates, headline flags
- Filing metadata (timestamp, source, sequence number)
