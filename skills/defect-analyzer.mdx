## What it does

The **Defect Analyzer** reviews a vessel's defect register, classifies findings by safety impact, identifies recurring equipment themes, and produces a senior review. It is the lens that turns a list of open items into prioritised action.

It pairs with the [Defect Data Collector](/skills/defect-data-collector): one fetches, one interprets.

## When to use it

- "Defect status?"
- "Equipment concerns?"
- "Any open safety items?"
- "Run defect review"
- "Safety items on AQUILA"

## What it looks at

- Open defect count by criticality
- Age of each open defect (especially &gt;90 days)
- Equipment / system distribution (recurring themes)
- Closure rate over time
- Defects with safety, class, or PSC implications
- Stuck defects (waiting on parts, port call, vendor)

## How it works

### 1. Severity classification

Each defect is classified:

| Class | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| CRITICAL | Safety equipment, class condition, or PSC-flagging item |
| MAJOR | Operationally significant — affects performance or compliance |
| MINOR | Routine maintenance backlog |

### 2. Age analysis

Age tiers identify systemic issues:

- **&gt;180 days open** — chronic, raises question on closure process
- **90–180 days** — overdue
- **30–90 days** — active
- **&lt;30 days** — recent

A spike of CRITICAL items older than 90 days is a major escalation trigger.

### 3. Equipment-theme clustering

The analyzer groups open defects by equipment family. Three or more defects on the same system points to a systemic issue, not isolated failures.

### 4. Closure-rate trend

Closure rate is tracked period-on-period:

$$
\text{Closure rate} = \frac{\text{Defects closed in period}}{\text{Open at start} + \text{Opened in period}}
$$

A closure rate falling for 2+ periods combined with a rising open count signals operational drift — the team is opening defects faster than they're closing them.

## What the review contains

- Executive summary with overall defect posture
- Severity distribution and age breakdown
- Equipment-theme clusters (likely systemic issues)
- Stuck defects with blocker categorised (parts, port, vendor)
- Closure-rate trend
- Recommendations prioritised by safety impact and age
- Escalation decision

## Escalation

Escalates to a Technical Superintendent automatically when:

| Trigger | Severity |
|---------|----------|
| CRITICAL defect open &gt;30 days | CRITICAL |
| Safety-equipment defect open at all | CRITICAL |
| Closure rate falling for 2+ periods | HIGH |
| Equipment cluster (3+ defects same system) | HIGH |
| Class condition unresolved past deadline | CRITICAL |
