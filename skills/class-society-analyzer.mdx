## What it does

The **Class Society Analyzer** examines a vessel's class survey portfolio — overall status, individual surveys, conditions of class, outstanding findings — and produces a senior compliance review. It evaluates due-window pressure, finding severity, recurring deficiency patterns, and **PSC detention risk** stemming from class issues.

It pairs with the [Class Society Data Collector](/skills/class-society-data). The collector scrapes the class portal; the analyzer interprets what was found.

## When to use it

- "How are surveys?"
- "Class status on AQUILA"
- "Any PSC risk from surveys?"
- "Run class review"
- "Survey compliance check"

## What it looks at

- Overall class status (in-class, recommendations, conditions)
- Each survey's due date, completion state, and credit status
- Conditions of class with deadlines
- Outstanding and recently closed findings
- Asset (machinery / equipment) condition flags
- Recurring deficiency themes across surveys

## How it works

### 1. Survey portfolio mapping

Every survey in the portfolio is bucketed by status:

- **OVERDUE** — past due date, vessel non-compliant
- **DUE SOON** — within 30 days
- **PLANNED** — 30–90 days, port-call coordination
- **CURRENT** — credited and within window

### 2. Findings analysis

The script clusters findings by:

- Severity (critical / major / minor)
- Equipment / system affected
- Status (open, closed, follow-up required)
- Age (how long open)

Old open findings and clusters of repeat issues on the same equipment are flagged as systemic concerns — not individual incidents.

### 3. Conditions of class

Each Condition of Class is tracked against its deadline. A condition approaching its window adds a multiplier to detention risk because failure to clear it suspends class.

### 4. PSC detention-risk score

A weighted composite combines five contributing factors:

$$
\text{Risk score} = w_1 S_\text{overdue} + w_2 F_\text{critical} + w_3 C_\text{cond} + w_4 R_\text{recur} + w_5 P_\text{psc}
$$

Where $S_\text{overdue}$ is overdue surveys, $F_\text{critical}$ is open critical findings, $C_\text{cond}$ is conditions of class within 30 days, $R_\text{recur}$ is recurring deficiency themes, and $P_\text{psc}$ is recent PSC inspection density. The composite maps to LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH / CRITICAL bands.

## What the review contains

- Executive summary with overall class status
- Survey table — each survey's status, due date, credit
- Findings analysis — severity distribution, age, recurring themes
- Conditions of class — deadlines and remediation plan
- PSC detention-risk assessment with contributing factors
- Recommendations prioritized by deadline and impact
- Escalation decision

## Escalation

The analyzer escalates to a Technical Superintendent automatically when:

| Trigger | Severity |
|---------|----------|
| Any survey overdue | CRITICAL |
| Condition of class within 30 days unresolved | CRITICAL |
| Open critical finding older than 60 days | HIGH |
| Recurring deficiency theme (3+ findings on same system) | HIGH |
| Class status downgraded | CRITICAL |

## Why script-driven

Survey-state logic, age clustering, and risk-score weighting all live in the analysis script — not the language model. Reviews stay consistent across vessels and audits.
