## What it does

The **Water Treatment Analyzer** reviews water chemistry across a vessel's systems — boiler water, cooling water, potable water, fresh-water generator output. It evaluates parameter compliance with maker spec, identifies trends pointing to equipment risk (chloride ingress, scaling, microbiological contamination), and produces a senior review.

It pairs with the [Water Treatment Data Collector](/skills/water-treatment-data-collector): one fetches, one interprets.

## When to use it

- "Water chemistry OK?"
- "Boiler condition?"
- "Check cooling water"
- "FWG salinity"
- "Lab review for water"

## What it looks at

- Boiler water: pH, conductivity, hardness, phosphate, chloride
- Cooling water: pH, conductivity, nitrite, chloride, microbial
- Potable water: chlorine, hardness, microbial, salinity
- Fresh-water generator output: salinity, conductivity
- Chemical dosing rates and ROB
- Trend across the last three samples per system

## How it works

### 1. Parameter compliance

Each parameter is compared against the maker / equipment-specific spec. Out-of-range values are flagged WARNING; outside the wider safety envelope, CRITICAL.

### 2. Equipment damage risk

Specific patterns map to specific risks:

- Boiler chloride above limit → tube corrosion / failure
- Cooling chloride above limit → seawater ingress
- Cooling pH falling → general corrosion
- Microbial counts rising → biofilm and pitting
- FWG salinity rising → membrane / heat exchanger fault

### 3. Dosing review

Chemical ROB and dosing rates are evaluated against expected consumption. Under-dosing or running out is a near-term equipment risk.

### 4. Trend assessment

Three-sample trend per parameter classifies systems as Improving / Stable / Deteriorating, regardless of whether the latest value is in spec. Trend slope is computed as:

$$
\text{slope} = \frac{x_n - x_{n-2}}{2 \times \Delta t}
$$

A positive slope on an upper-bounded parameter (chloride, conductivity) is flagged Deteriorating; the same on a lower-bounded parameter (pH, nitrite) is flagged Improving. Direction matters, not just magnitude.

## What the review contains

- Executive summary with overall water posture per system
- Per-system parameter table with verdict
- Out-of-range values with associated equipment risk
- Dosing review and chemical ROB
- Trend per parameter
- Recommendations (dosing change, equipment inspection, sample re-run)
- Escalation decision

## Escalation

Escalates to the Technical Superintendent when:

| Trigger | Severity |
|---------|----------|
| Boiler chloride above critical limit | CRITICAL |
| Cooling chloride above critical limit (seawater ingress suspected) | CRITICAL |
| FWG salinity rising trend | HIGH |
| Microbial count above safety limit | HIGH |
| Chemical out of stock with no resupply planned | HIGH |
