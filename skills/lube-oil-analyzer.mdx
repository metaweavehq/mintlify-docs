## What it does

The **Lube Oil Analyzer** turns lube oil lab results into an early-warning indicator for engine condition. Rising wear metals, falling TBN, water ingress, and viscosity drift are the first signs of bearing wear, fuel dilution, or coolant leakage — well before they become engine failures. The analyzer reads the latest lab batch, compares it against trends, and produces a senior review.

It pairs with the [Lube Oil Data Collector](/skills/lube-oil-data-collector): one fetches, one interprets.

## When to use it

- "LO trends?"
- "Wear metals OK?"
- "Lube oil review"

## What it looks at

- Wear metals: iron, copper, lead, tin, chromium, aluminium
- Additive metals: zinc, calcium, magnesium, phosphorus
- Contaminants: water, fuel dilution, soot, silicon (dirt ingress)
- Oil condition: viscosity at 40 °C and 100 °C, TBN, TAN, oxidation
- Trend across the last three samples per equipment

## How it works

### 1. Wear-metal trends

For each metal the script computes period-over-period change and flags:

- Trend rising consistently
- Latest value above maker's caution threshold
- Step change vs prior sample (sudden spike)

The rate of change per metal:

$$
\dot{m} = \frac{m_n - m_{n-1}}{\Delta t}
$$

A step jump where $m_n > 2 \times m_{n-1}$ is treated as an event, not noise.

### 2. Contamination flags

Water above the threshold for the engine type; fuel dilution above 5%; silicon above 25 ppm — each is a distinct fault category with different root causes.

### 3. Oil condition

TBN reserve is tracked against the fresh-oil baseline:

$$
\text{TBN reserve \%} = \frac{\text{TBN}_\text{current}}{\text{TBN}_\text{new}} \times 100
$$

A reserve below 50% triggers oil-change discussion; below 25% is treated as overdue. Viscosity drift outside the maker's spec indicates fuel dilution or oxidation.

### 4. Equipment-pattern correlation

The analyzer cross-references trends with equipment running hours and recent overhauls — wear after a recent overhaul means break-in; sustained wear well into a service interval means accelerated degradation.

## What the review contains

- Executive summary with overall LO posture
- Per-equipment trend table (wear metals, additives, contaminants)
- Contamination findings with likely cause
- TBN and viscosity status
- Equipment correlation (recent overhaul, running hours)
- Recommendations (oil change, equipment inspection, fuel investigation)
- Escalation decision

## Escalation

Escalates to the Technical Superintendent when:

| Trigger | Severity |
|---------|----------|
| Wear metal above CRITICAL threshold | CRITICAL |
| Sudden step change in any wear metal | HIGH |
| Water above engine spec | HIGH |
| Fuel dilution above 5% | CRITICAL |
| TBN below 25% of new oil | HIGH |
